Two open-source relatives, different jobs

Looking for an EasyEngine alternative?

EasyEngine and SpawnWP are the closest kind of relatives: both self-hosted, both Docker-based, both open source. The difference is the job. EasyEngine manages persistent WordPress sites from a CLI; SpawnWP spawns disposable, isolated development environments from a web cockpit — and everything in it, cockpit included, is MIT-licensed.

Competitor information checked July 17, 2026
The real difference

Persistent sites vs sacrificable environments.

EasyEngine is a mature command-line control panel for WordPress on Ubuntu: it creates and operates long-lived, Docker-backed sites with nginx, SSL and caching. Its CLI is MIT-licensed; a separate web dashboard product offers free and paid plans by site count.

SpawnWP assumes your sites are temporary. It turns a fresh Debian or Ubuntu server into a lab where each environment lives in its own container stack, is created from a blueprint in a web cockpit, carries WordPress.org QA tooling preinstalled, and is destroyed without ceremony when the work is done.

Make the choice explicit

Let the lifecycle decide.

Choose EasyEngine if

Your sites are permanent

You operate long-lived WordPress sites — production included — and prefer a battle-tested CLI workflow on Ubuntu.

Choose SpawnWP if

Your sites are experiments

You spawn environments to build, test or demo something and want them isolated, QA-equipped and easy to destroy — from a browser.

Common questions

Before you pick a relative.

Is SpawnWP a fork of EasyEngine?

No. They share the self-hosted, Docker-based, open-source model but are independent projects with different goals: EasyEngine manages persistent sites, SpawnWP spawns disposable development environments.

Is the SpawnWP web interface open source too?

Yes. The entire project, including the web cockpit, is MIT-licensed with no paid plans or site limits. EasyEngine's CLI is MIT-licensed, while its web dashboard is a separate product with free and paid plans.

Which one should host production sites?

EasyEngine. SpawnWP is deliberately a development, testing and demo lab and does not aim to be a production hosting stack.

Official sources: easyengine.io and the EasyEngine GitHub repository (MIT license). Features and plans can change; verify them before deciding.

Ready to operate the lab?

Check the server requirements first.
